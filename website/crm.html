The honest answer

Do you actually need
a CRM?

It's one of the first things business owners ask us, and it deserves a straight answer. Sometimes the answer is yes — buy one. But roughly half of all CRM projects fail, and they rarely fail because the software is bad. They fail because it was never shaped around the way the business actually runs.

Underneath the feature lists

Every CRM is doing the same four jobs.

Worth knowing, because it tells you exactly what you're paying a monthly fee for.

Remember the customer

Contacts, history, notes — one place instead of six phones and a notebook.

Track the work

A pipeline or job list, so nothing sits half-finished and forgotten.

Chase automatically

Reminders, follow-ups, triggers — the admin you keep meaning to do.

Tell you the truth

What's coming in, what's converting, and where it stalls.

Those four jobs are worth having. The question was never whether you need them — it's whether your business should be reshaped to fit someone else's version of them.

Rented vs owned

A CRM holds your customer list.

That's the part worth sitting with. The single most valuable asset most small businesses have is who their customers are and what they buy — and in a rented system, that lives on someone else's platform.

A rented CRM

Built for everyone. Shaped like no one.

  • You fit your process around its pipeline
  • Your customer list lives on their platform
  • You pay per user, every month, forever
  • The bill climbs as your team grows
  • Stop paying and you're left with an export file

A system you own

Built around exactly how you work.

  • The workflow is yours — nothing to un-learn
  • Your data is yours, and stays yours
  • A flat, predictable cost as you scale
  • Adding staff doesn't add to the bill
  • Walk away any time and take it all with you

Often the two aren't even rivals.

A rented CRM and the system we build can sit happily as different layers — keep the tool that already works, and we'll build the part that doesn't exist yet.

Straight answers

When you should just buy one.

Your process is genuinely standard

A generic pipeline fits it and you don't have to bend anything to make it work. The lightweights above are good value for exactly this.

You need something running this week

Speed matters more than fit right now, and that's a fair call. A bespoke build is measured in weeks, not days.

You're testing an idea

Don't build around a process you haven't settled yet — prove the business first, then build around what it turned out to be.

If off-the-shelf software already does everything you need, we'll be the first to say so. We're here for the businesses it doesn't quite fit — and you'll know which one you are within about ten minutes of talking to us.
